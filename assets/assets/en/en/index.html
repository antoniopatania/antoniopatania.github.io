An inner inquiry runs through Antonio Patania’s paintings: it emerges in faces, gathers in the gaze, and shields itself behind armors of strength and fear.

My painting practice is rooted in a dialogue between matter and inner life. I work on human figures—especially female faces—as sites of emotional identification. Layers of oil, wax, earth pigments, oxides, and at times gold leaf veil the image, filtering reality and turning it into an inner experience. Every face becomes a mirror: I cannot paint it unless it moves through me.
